Shmita Table

This app displays the shmita status of each species of fruit and vegetable based on the current date. Fruits are displayed in purple text, and vegetables in green text. Other foods (like grains and spices) are in black.

In addition, you can download a calendar file, which can be imported to Google Calendar or another calendar program. It will alert you whenever the status of a food changes.

A brief overview of the statuses:

  • Kedushat shviit ("Kedushah"): Produce which grew in the Land of Israel during the shmita year. This produce must be eaten rather than wasted or destroyed. There are also restrictions on how you can farm and buy/sell this produce. Therefore, when a species has Kedushah, you must make sure to either obtain and consume it in acceptable ways (like "otzar beit din"), or else rely on a loophole (like "heter mechira" or "yevul nochri") so that the actual produce you buy does not have Kedushah.
  • Sfichin: Produce which began growing (was planted) during the shmita year. It is forbidden to eat such produce, unless it uses a loophole like "heter mechira" or "yevul nochri".
  • Biur: Produce with Kedushah ("otzar beit din") must be physically removed from one's home after a certain date, if you still have them then.

For a full explanation of the shmita laws, see here.
